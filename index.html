Food Insecurity and Obesity

Food insecurity was associated with obesity in the overall population and most population subgroups. These findings are consistent with previous research and highlight the importance of increasing access to affordable healthy foods for all adults. This examines the relationship between food insecurity and obesity in Georgia, by County.

About Caitlin

Caitlin graduated from the University of Kentucky in 2016 with a B.S. in Kinesiology. She continued her education at UK and Johns Hopkins University in Biostatistics, Public Health Management, and Epidemiology. Her current research interests include decision science, patterns of cancer care, health disparities, social determinants of health, access to care, and healthcare organizations and environments.
